About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, specializing in Deep Learning for Heathcare. My research focuses on developing deep learning methods for Clinical Trials. My academic journey combines teaching and research. I served as a full-time faculty member at Brac University for ~2 years and worked as a Teaching Assistant (TA) at UIUC for multiple courses.

My work focuses on advancing clinical trial research with deep learning, addressing key challenges such as the lack of patient-level data due to privacy concerns, the absence of labeled trial outcomes, and the labor-intensive process of identifying similar historical trialsâ€”an essential step for optimizing trial design and improving trial success rates. I have developed methods for synthetic patient data generation to mitigate data scarcity, a benchmark dataset for trial outcome labeling using weak supervision, and a novel approach to trial similarity search leveraging contrastive learning.
